Our System

The Colours Renamed

Each colour was renamed by blending words from two Afrikan languages, prioritising meaning over visuals. Rather than using Western colour terms, the names draw from our chosen tribes.

Tiziri-Duhu Black

The Break Down

Tiziri (Amazigh: the dark that holds light)

Duhu (Swahili root: deep darkness)

Meaning

Depth, mystery, the cosmos, the ancestors.

Fari-Timεt White

The Break Down

Fari (Hausa: white/purity)

Timεt (Amazigh: identity/being)

Meaning

Purification, spiritual clarity, ancestral presence. White as self-knowledge not absence.

Mogya-Ja Red

The Break Down

Mogya (Akan: blood)

Ja (Hausa: red/passion)

Meaning

Blood, life force, sacrifice, power. The colour that moves across almost every African colour tradition as the pivot point.

Sika-Warabe Yellow

The Break Down

Sika (Akan: gold/wealth)

Warabe (Oromo: radiance/celebration)

Meaning

Gold, harvest, the sun at its height, abundance.

Kore-Uhlaza Green

The Break Down

Kore (Hausa: green/vegetation)

Uhlaza (Zulu/Ndebele: green/living things)

Meaning

Growth, land, healing, everything that grows from the earth.

Buluu-Amanzi Blue

The Break Down

Buluu (Hausa: blue, borrowed and indigenised)

Amanzi (Zulu/Ndebele: water)

Meaning

Sky, water — blue and green as one continuous living field.

Our Indexing System

Our indexing system makes use of cultural and psychological meaning directly into colour references, replacing the Western RGB model with a framework rooted in Afrikan knowledge systems.

The Format — Value Culture Meaning Origin

Value

The value represents the strength of the colour. Black, being a colour representing strength, is mixed into each tribe's base colour to represent the strengthening of the meaning. This creates a tonal range within each tribe's palette, allowing designers to work with cultural depth without breaking the system.

Culture

One tribe from each region of Afrika, shortened into three letters.

East Afrika ORM = Oromo
West Afrika HSA = Hausa
North Afrika AMZ = Amazigh
South Afrika NDB = Ndebele
Central Afrika BWA = Batwa

Meaning

The meanings were categorised and numbers were assigned to them according to Afrikan numerology.

Conflict [2] Wrath, Danger, Misfortune, Social Transgression
Emotion [3] Hope, Peace, Stability, Harmony, Prosperity, Abundance
Nature [4] Land, Nature, Harvest, Sunlight, Paradise, Mountains, Mediterranean Sea
Life [5] Life, Vitality, Energy, Wealth, Growth, Fertility, Warmth, Celebration
Spiritual [7] Soul, Purity, Mystery, Spirituality, Divine Light, Spiritual Agency, Spiritual Cleansing, Ancestral Presence, Spiritual Connection
Power [8] Blood, Power, Prestige, Sacrifice, Historical Resistance
Virtue [9] Unity, Identity, Bravery, Survival, Strength, Protection, Endurance, Trustworthiness

Origin

Each colour has its physical origin. These origins were categorised into Mineral [M], Vegetation [V], Insect [I] and Clay [C].

M
Mineral
Ochre, soot, iron-rich earth, charcoal, malachite
V
Vegetation
Indigofera, onion skins, fig leaves, madder root, turmeric
I
Insect
Cochineal — used for deep reds across East Afrikan traditions
C
Clay
Kaolin clay, chalk — used for whites, creams, spiritual body paint

Why Did We Reinvent The Indexing System?

Every time a designer uses a colour, they're engaging with a specific Afrikan tribe's knowledge, psychological and spiritual meaning, historical production methods and tonal depth within cultural context.
