Our Colour System Applied

Moenie baiza nie — township slang for don't fail, stand your ground — is a saying carried by communities like Eldorado Park, long shaped by gun and gang violence. The smoke left after a gun is fired became the form: a fluid, organic object that transforms an instrument of harm into a symbol of resilience. The three images below show the Hausa colour system applied to that same form — because colour is never neutral. It carries memory, identity, and meaning, and in the right hands it can turn something heavy into something that holds people up.
